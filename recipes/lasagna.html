Lasagna

Description

A recipe from my home in Emilia-Romagna, Italy. This lasagne verde made with spinach pasta cannot be beaten. It's excellent if you want to impress some guests — or even yourself. It may be a bit time-consuming, but it's well worth the TLC you put into it.

Ingredients

  • 5 ounces spinach - rinsed, stemmed, and dried
  • ⅝ cup semolina flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 stalk celery, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 slices bacon, diced
  • 3 ½ ounces lean ground pork
  • 3 ½ ounces lean ground beef
  • 3 ½ ounces minced ham
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ ounces chicken livers, trimmed and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups warm milk
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 1 ⅔ cups grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tablespoons butter

Steps

  1. Place a steamer insert into a saucepan.
  2. Fill with water to just below the bottom of the steamer.
  3. Bring water to a boil.
  4. Add spinach, cover.
  5. Steam until bright green, about 2 minutes.
  6. Squeeze spinach to remove excess moisture.
  7. Process in a food processor to make a paste
  8. Add semolina, eggs, and salt.
  9. Process until smooth.
  10. Transfer mixture to a large bowl
  11. Stir in enough flour to make a smooth dough
  12. Knead briefly.
  13. Cover with a dish towel.
  14. Set aside.
  15. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium-high heat.
  16. Cook and stir carrot, celery, onion, and bacon in hot butter until onion is translucent.
  17. Stir in pork, beef, and ham.
  18. Cook and stir until crumbly and browned
  19. tir in beef stock, tomato paste, and oregano.
  20. Season with salt and pepper.
  21. Reduce heat to low
  22. Cover
  23. Simmer for 20 minutes
  24. Combine butter and flour in a medium saucepan over medium-low heat.
  25. Whisk to make a roux.
  26. Remove from heat
  27. Let rest for 1 minute
  28. Whisk in warm milk
  29. Return to heat
  30. Simmer for 10 minutes
  31. Stir constantly
  32. Season with salt and nutmeg.
  33. Remove from heat.
  34. Prepare an ice bath.
  35. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
  36. On a floured surface, divide pasta dough into 3 portions.
  37. Roll each portion out to a thin sheet.
  38. Cook each pasta sheet in boiling water for 3 minutes.
  39. Remove and dip in ice water.
  40. Drain and dry on a clean cloth.
  41. Stir chicken livers into simmering sauce.
  42. Cook for 1 minute.
  43. Remove from heat.
  44. Set aside.
  45. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F
  46. Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  47. Place 1 pasta sheet in the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  48. Spreadover pasta:
    • 1/3 of the ragu
    • 1/4 of the béchamel
    • 1/3 of the ricotta
    • 1/4 of the Parmesan
  49. Repeat layers twice
  50. Top with remaining béchamel and Parmesan.
  51. Dot with butter.
  52. Bake in the preheated oven until the top is golden brown, about 30 minutes.
